The Problem

Most hosts are optimizing blind.

You're putting in the effort: the pricing tool, the photos, the tweaks. But without the right data, you're guessing. And that guessing costs you thousands every year.

01
No visibility into your ranking position
Most ranking and pricing tools give you a blended average and call it a day. Nobody is showing you where you actually appear for each open night, across different guest counts and booking windows.
02
Your photos are no longer shown in the order you set them
Airbnb's algorithm now dynamically reorders your photos based on what guests are searching for. A guest filtering for a pool may see your pool photo first, regardless of your intended order. That means photo strategy can no longer focus on just the cover image. Every photo across your entire listing needs to perform on its own.
03
No real understanding of your competitive landscape
Pricing in isolation only tells half the story. Without monitoring the supply, availability, and pricing of similar listings in your market, you have no way to know when to compete aggressively on price or when to surge because real supply has dried up.
04
Promotions without a strategy behind them
Running a discount is easy. Knowing whether it should be 20% or 60% is the hard part. The size of a promotion directly impacts where you rank, and most hosts have no data to guide that decision. Too small and it does nothing for visibility. Too large and you leave revenue on the table.
05
Incomplete listing, incomplete results
A listing is more than a title, description, and a few amenities. The back-end settings, rule configurations, and platform features that most hosts overlook are some of the biggest quiet drivers of search performance. The more completely you fill out your listing, the more the algorithm rewards you.
